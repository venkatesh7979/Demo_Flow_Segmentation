Harmonic Flows

Until now we were mostly dealing with the complex flows which only have local phenomena i.e. elementary flows will have diminishing effect as we move away from their center. Now we can work on the flows which have global phenomena where effects due to the elementary flows can be seen everywhere in the complex flow field.

Harmonic flows comprise the elementary flows that can be formulated with the sinusoidal velocity functions with various frequencies. Each sinusoidal function has a different amplitude and its only space-variant. These flows can be considered to be inviscid and in-compressible. A complex flow based on the Harmonic flows can be formed by superimposing various velocity fields that are made of sinusoidal functions.
